üéØ CW Operating Procedures

Standard Q-Signals

Essential Q-codes used in amateur radio CW operations:

  • QRL? - Is this frequency busy?
  • QRM - Man-made interference
  • QRN - Static/atmospheric noise
  • QRS - Send slower
  • QRT - Stop transmitting
  • QRZ? - Who is calling me?
  • QSB - Signal fading
  • QSL - I acknowledge/QSL card
  • QSO - Two-way communication
  • QSY - Change frequency
  • QTH - Location/address

üèÜ Contest Reference

Popular CW Contests

Major contests that Head Copy helps you practice for:

CQ WPX Contest

  • Exchange: RST + Serial Number
  • Focus: Prefixes and call sign diversity
  • When: Last full weekends of March and October

CWops CWT (CW Test)

  • Exchange: Name + State/Province/Country
  • When: Wednesday, Thursday (1300Z, 1900Z, 0300Z)
  • Duration: 1 hour

ARRL Slow Speed Test (SST)

  • Exchange: RST + State/Province/DX
  • Speed: Maximum 20 WPM
  • Perfect for beginners

CWops Mini-Speed Test (MST)

  • Exchange: Name + Serial Number
  • When: Multiple times weekly
  • Duration: 30 minutes

NCCC NS Sprint Ladder

  • Exchange: Serial + Name + State/Province
  • Fast-paced sprint format
  • When: Weekly NS events

üí° Learning Tips & Techniques

The Koch Method

Start with full speed characters but shorter combinations. Master characters at target speed, then increase length.

Farnsworth Timing

Learn characters at high speed (18+ WPM) but with extra spacing between characters. Gradually reduce spacing.

Practice Progression

  • Start with individual characters
  • Move to random character groups
  • Practice common words
  • Learn standard abbreviations
  • Practice callsigns
  • Try QSO simulation
  • Enter actual contests

RST System

Standard signal reporting system for CW:

Readability (R)

  • 1 - Unreadable
  • 2 - Barely readable
  • 3 - Readable with difficulty
  • 4 - Readable with no difficulty
  • 5 - Perfectly readable

Signal Strength (S)

  • 1 - Faint signals, barely perceptible
  • 5 - Fairly good signals
  • 9 - Extremely strong signals

Tone (T)

  • 1 - Sixty cycle AC or less, very rough and broad
  • 5 - Rough AC tone, rectified but not filtered
  • 9 - Perfect DC tone, pure and chirpless

Common Reports: 599 (perfect), 579 (good), 559 (fair)

üìª CW Band Plans

HF CW Frequencies

Primary CW portions of amateur bands:

  • 80m: 3.500 - 3.600 MHz
  • 40m: 7.000 - 7.125 MHz
  • 20m: 14.000 - 14.070 MHz
  • 15m: 21.000 - 21.070 MHz
  • 10m: 28.000 - 28.070 MHz

QRS (Slow Speed) Frequencies

  • 80m: 3.555 MHz
  • 40m: 7.055 MHz
  • 20m: 14.055 MHz

Contest Activity

During contests, activity spreads across entire CW subbands. Popular contest frequencies include the lower portions of each band.
