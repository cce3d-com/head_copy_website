Quick Start Checklist

  1. Adjust Audio Settings: Go to Settings and configure tone frequency and volume
  2. Set Your Speed: Start with 15-20 WPM for beginners, 25-30 WPM for experienced operators
  3. Choose Your First Mode: Try "Random Words" for general practice or "Callsign Trainer" for amateur radio specific training
  4. Practice Daily: Even 10-15 minutes daily is more effective than longer, infrequent sessions

Essential Prosigns

Basic Prosigns

  • AR: End of message
  • SK: End of contact
  • BT: Break, pause
  • KN: Go ahead (specific station only)

Contest Prosigns

  • TU: Thank you
  • AGN: Again
  • QRL?: Is frequency busy?
  • QRT: Stopping transmission

Number Formats

  • Cut Numbers: Contest abbreviations (T=0, N=9, A=1, etc.)
  • Standard Numbers: Full number transmission
  • RST Reports: 599, 579, etc.
  • Serial Numbers: Contest exchange numbers

üí° Cut Numbers: Learn cut numbers for contest operation. They save time and reduce errors when sending serial numbers quickly.

üí° Tips & Tricks

Learning Strategies

üß† Koch Method

Learn characters at full speed but with extended spacing. This builds proper character recognition from the start.

‚è∞ Short Sessions

15-20 minute sessions are more effective than marathon practice. Your brain needs time to process.

üéØ Focus on Accuracy

Maintain 90%+ accuracy before increasing speed. Quality over quantity always wins.

üìö Mixed Practice

Alternate between different modes: words, callsigns, and contests for well-rounded skills.

Speed Building

  • Plateau Breaking: When stuck, increase speed by 5 WPM for short bursts, then return to target speed
  • Character Speed: Keep character speed higher than overall speed (Farnsworth method)
  • Comfort Zone: Practice at your target speed, not your comfortable speed
  • Weak Characters: Identify and focus on problem letters or numbers

Contest Preparation

  • Exchange Practice: Know contest exchanges by heart before contest weekend
  • Speed Margin: Practice 5-10 WPM faster than your planned contest speed
  • Noise Tolerance: Use Head Copy's band noise simulation for realistic practice
  • Operating Procedures: Practice proper contest CQ timing and exchange procedures

‚ö†Ô∏è Avoid Burnout: Take breaks every 20-30 minutes. Mental fatigue leads to poor learning and frustration. Quality practice time is more valuable than quantity.

üîß Troubleshooting

Audio Issues

üîá No Sound

  • Check device volume and mute switch
  • Verify app volume in Settings
  • Close and restart the app
  • Restart your device

üéµ Distorted Audio

  • Lower the tone frequency
  • Reduce app volume
  • Check for Bluetooth interference
  • Try different waveform settings

‚è±Ô∏è Timing Problems

  • Close background apps
  • Disable Low Power Mode
  • Update to latest iOS version
  • Restart the device

üéß Headphone Issues

  • Try different headphones
  • Check for loose connections
  • Test with device speakers
  • Reset Bluetooth connections

App Performance

  • Slow Response: Close other apps, restart Head Copy, ensure sufficient storage space
  • Crashes: Update the app, restart device, contact support if persistent
  • Battery Drain: Disable background app refresh, reduce screen brightness, use Low Power Mode
  • Sync Issues: Check internet connection, sign out and back into App Store

Subscription Problems

  • Purchase Not Working: Verify payment method, check App Store restrictions
  • Features Not Unlocked: Restart app, restore purchases in Settings
  • Billing Questions: Contact Apple Support or check App Store purchase history
  • Cancel Subscription: Manage subscriptions in iOS Settings > Apple ID > Subscriptions
